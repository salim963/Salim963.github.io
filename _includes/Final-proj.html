Fire battalions in San Francisco

Battalions are geographic regions within the San Francisco Fire Department's coverage area. Each Battalion have many fire stations is responsible for responding to emergency calls within its assigned area, and is made up of multiple fire stations and firefighting personnel. There are 10 Battalions in San Francisco, and they are identified by a unique code starting with the letter 'B', followed by a two-digit number.

The plot shows the total number of calls for all 10 Battalions in San Francisco by year. From the chart, we can see that the number of calls has been increasing gradually over the years, with the year 2022 having the highest number of calls.

To get a closer look at the data, we can focus on the year 2022, which had the most calls. This finding is crucial for the fire department to allocate their resources effectively and be better prepared to respond to emergencies. It could also indicate the need for additional education and awareness campaigns in the community to prevent emergencies from occurring in the first place.

Average arrival time for each neighborhood in 2022

In addition to identifying the most common types of fire calls in San Francisco, it's also important to consider how quickly the fire department is able to respond to these emergencies.

That's why we've created a plot that shows the average response time for four different types of calls in various neighborhoods throughout the city.

The four types of calls we're focusing on are Outside Rubbish Fire, False Alarm/Call, Cooking Fire, and 'Building Fire. By analyzing the response times for these calls, we can get a better understanding of how well-equipped the fire department is to handle different types of emergencies in different neighborhoods.

As expected, the average response times vary significantly depending on the type of call and the neighborhood in question. For example, the average response time in 12/2022 for Noe valley neighborhood is only 3 minutes and 25 seconds, whereas the average response time for Golden Gate Park neighborhood is 6 minutes and 40 seconds.


But what does delay at response times means?

Average response times are an important metric for emergency services such as fire departments because they directly impact the outcomes of emergency incidents. In the case of fires, every minute of delay in response time can mean the difference between life and death, as well as significant property damage.

For example, a delay of just a few minutes can cause a small fire to grow into a large blaze, making it more difficult and dangerous for firefighters to contain. In addition, every minute spent responding to a false alarm or non-emergency incident is time taken away from potentially life-saving emergencies.

By comparing the response times across different neighborhoods, we can identify areas where the fire department may need to allocate more resources or adjust their response strategies. Additionally, we can use this data to better understand the relationship between call type, neighborhood, and response time.

This plot works hand in hand with the map we previously created, which shows the distribution of these call types across different neighborhoods in San Francisco. By combining these two visualizations, we can gain a more comprehensive understanding of how the fire department is handling emergencies throughout the city.
For example we can conclude from the map and the plot the there is a need for fire station in Golden Gate Park especially at the corner for the border between Inner Richmond and Haight Ashbury.

When Are Fire Departments the Busiest?

We have analyzed the hourly counts of fire incidents calls received by the fire department.
The data clearly shows that the majority of fire incidents occur during the day, with the highest number of calls received between 9:00 to 21:00. Specifically, there is a peak in fire calls between 17:00 to 19:00, indicating that this is a particularly busy time for the fire department.

On the other hand, we can see that the lowest count of calls occurs between 3:00 to 6:00. This is likely due to the fact that people are sleeping during these hours, and there are fewer activities that could potentially cause fires.

Based on these observations, we can conclude that the fire department should focus their resources and efforts during the busiest hours of the day.

By doing so, they can better serve the community and ensure that they are able to respond to emergency calls as quickly as possible.
